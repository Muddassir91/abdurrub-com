Oshun

A full email engine built before launch.

A new goat milk soap brand had a product, a small waitlist, and zero email infrastructure. I owned strategy, copy, and the Klaviyo flow architecture from scratch.

30+Launch emails and core lifecycle flows scoped, written, and handed off.

The problem.

Most new skincare brands copy the clean-girl category template. Oshun needed a retention system that felt like Oshun before the first real buying data existed. The work started with product story, founder direction, customer-language research, and a clear decision on voice.

StackKlaviyo and Shopify.
StagePre-launch through post-purchase.
OwnershipStrategy, copy, flow map, subject lines, and image direction.

The build.

I built two voice tracks first: one funny and goat-forward, one premium and sensory-led. The founder picked the irreverent direction. From there I mapped the full launch and retention layer: pre-launch waitlist, welcome, browse abandonment, cart abandonment, checkout abandonment, post-purchase, subscription renewal, re-engagement, VIP, and referral.

The handoff.

Every flow shipped as a build-ready Google Doc with subject lines, preview text, body copy, and image direction. The team also got a flow map, trigger logic, exclusions, timing, a subject-line bank, and a short test plan for what to A/B once data started coming in.

The brand did not need a skincare template. It needed a voice customers could remember.

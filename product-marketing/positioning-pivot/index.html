Strategy · Positioning · You Ai

Repositioned the You Ai outcome promise around revenue brackets after research showed the original was misaligned

The original You Ai positioning told the ideal customer they could go from $100K to $10M with a single product. Cross-checked it against how the high-ticket world actually segments by revenue. Nobody was making promises that big inside a single product. We changed it.

Co-led repositioning. One outsized promise → four tier-specific offers + a Passion.io downsell path.

The problem the team and I surfaced together

The early You Ai positioning landed on a single, ambitious outcome: take a coach or creator from $100K to $10M with one product. The number was inspiring and easy to write copy against. It also did not match how prospects in that revenue range actually buy.

I cross-checked the promise against how Hormozi, King Kong, and other established players in the high-ticket world were segmenting their offers. None of them were making promises that big inside a single product. The pattern was much smaller, segmented promises tied to the specific revenue bracket the prospect was already in.

How the research surfaced the gap

  • Sales-call review. The transcripts from twenty-plus discovery and sales calls showed prospects struggling with the $100K-to-$10M framing. Buyers under $300K could not picture the path. Buyers over $1M wanted specifics about scale, not foundation.
  • Competitive scan. I went through the offers and ad creative of the high-ticket coaching and consulting brands serving similar audiences. Every one of them ran tier-specific promises. Nobody bridged a 100× revenue jump in one program.
  • First-principles check. The math itself broke down. A single product cannot reasonably promise both a $300K founder and a $3M founder the same outcome. The constraints, the bottlenecks, and the next moves are not the same problem.

Single ambitious promise replaced by tier-specific promises plus a downsell path for prospects below the You Ai floor.

The fix

  • Subdivided the ideal customer profile by revenue bracket. $100K-$300K, $300K-$1M, $1M-$3M, $3M+. Each tier got its own primary outcome promise and its own messaging arc.
  • Added a downsell path. Sub-$100K founders began getting downsold to the Passion.io platform rather than disqualified. The platform was the right product for that tier; You Ai was not. The team built a system around this after I left.
  • Updated the messaging framework. The personas database, angles database, and outcome database all got refreshed to reflect the new tier structure. Every campaign downstream pulled from the new tiering.

Why this counts as product marketing work, not copy work

Copy could not have fixed this. The misalignment was at the offer-architecture layer, not the words layer. Surfacing it required reading sales transcripts against competitor offers, applying first-principles thinking to the math, and bringing the observation to a team conversation that included the founder, sales lead, and head of marketing.

The work that came out of it touched product packaging, sales qualification, and the cross-sell relationship between You Ai and the platform. That is positioning work.

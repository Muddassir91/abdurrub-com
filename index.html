Building and owning marketing operations.

The math behind retention

New customers are 5 to 7 times harder to convert than existing ones.

You already paid the hard cost. The ad, the funnel, the cold trust, all of that is spent. The cheapest revenue on the board is the next sale to a customer who already bought.

Source: Harvard Business Review · Bain & Company

Three signs

Who needs to book a retention strategy call.

1

Your revenue stopped climbing past $100K.

Your ads still convert. Your customers stop coming back. Every new sale costs you more than the last one did.

2

Your customers cancel inside 30 to 90 days.

Your product does what it promises. The relationship after the sale was never built, and you feel the leak before the dashboard shows it.

3

Your email list has thousands, barely used.

A working revenue channel is sitting dark in your CRM. The first sequence you actually send usually pays for the audit.

Common questions

The short answers.

What does a retention strategist actually do?
A retention strategist studies how customers move through your business, finds where revenue leaks, then builds the email and lifecycle work that keeps customers buying again. The output is a system the team runs after the engagement ends, not a one-off campaign.
Who do you work with?
Businesses already past $100K with a real customer base. SaaS, DTC, services, AI products. The systems travel across all of them.
How does the engagement start?
It starts with a free retention audit on a 20-minute call. Fit gets decided in the first ten minutes, not in a long pitch. If the audit surfaces real money on the table, the next step gets discussed on the same call.
Is this copywriting work?
Writing is part of the work, not the offer. The output is revenue from existing customers and leads. The inputs are research, positioning, lifecycle design, and direct response strategy.
How fast does this start showing up in revenue?
First wins usually land in the first 30 days from sequences already overdue. The compounding part takes 90 to 180 days, because retention is a curve, not a campaign.

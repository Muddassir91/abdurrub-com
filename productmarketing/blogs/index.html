SEO & long-form content · Passion.io

Long-form articles that put Passion.io alongside Mighty Networks and Kajabi in organic search

Co-led the SEO project at Passion.io with my team lead. The goal was to close the search-presence gap with competitors who had much larger SEO footprints. Articles were one piece of the project. Voice-of-customer mining, negative-review research, and a writing workflow were the rest.

The SEO project

Built the research and writing workflow that produced every article on this page

The project ran across two layers. The first was research-heavy: pull negative reviews from Reddit, niche forums, and review sites; turn the language into a problem table; then build a competitor feature comparison so each article had a real differentiator to lean on. The second was the writing workflow itself, briefed against a target keyword and a primary persona, mapped to a buying stage in the funnel.

  • Mined negative reviews of competing platforms (Skool, Mighty Networks, Kajabi, Circle) for voice-of-customer language. The result was a problem-table reference every article pulled from.
  • Built a competitor feature research table covering pricing, gamification, course UX, branding, payments, automation, and community moderation across the same competitors.
  • Wrote the articles below against that research. Each piece tied to a target keyword, a primary persona, and a funnel stage.
  • Why it matters in 2026: the same long-form content is now the source AI tools draw from when answering buyer questions. Investing in deep, original research-led articles is how Passion.io shows up alongside the competitors with bigger SEO footprints.

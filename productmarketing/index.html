Lifecycle · Largest line

Built and ran the webinar follow-up sequences that produced $1,115,407 in email revenue.

This was the single largest revenue line across the engagement. The sequences ran behind both live workshop funnels and evergreen webinars and were rebuilt as the product evolved. Most of the revenue came from segmentation and angle work pulled directly out of customer research. The same playbook scaled across launches.

Launch · Positioning

Owned the messaging framework and post-webinar follow-up for the YouAI launch.

YouAI is Passion.io's AI coach product. The launch ran through a live webinar funnel. I worked on the value proposition hierarchy, the launch landing page copy, and the marketing follow-up that turned webinar attendees into qualified leads. The AI Scaling Workshop signup page shown was the in-person workshop variant inside that funnel.

Strategy · Positioning

Surfaced the gap that the YouAI outcome promise was misaligned with how the market segments by revenue, and helped reposition it.

The original positioning told the ideal customer they could go from $100K to $10M with a single product. I cross-checked that against how Hormozi, King Kong, and other established players in the high-ticket world actually segmented their offers by revenue tier. Nobody else was making promises that big inside a single product.

I took the observation to the team. The ideal customer profile got subdivided into revenue brackets, and sub-$100K founders began getting downsold to the Passion.io platform rather than disqualified. The change made it into a system after I left.

Customer research

Built the customer research system that informed every messaging decision for the YouAI launch.

Reviewed more than twenty discovery and sales calls with target customers. Took transcripts. Watched facial cues, pulled vocal patterns, sorted pain points by intensity, and mapped objections to the moment they showed up in the funnel. Cross-checked the language against Reddit threads, niche forums, and creator-economy social channels to widen the sample.

Output was three customer personas with quoted limiting beliefs sorted by buying stage, plus an ever-expanding messaging rules document the team wrote against.

Internal systems

Built the six Notion databases that the marketing team's messaging strategy ran on for two years.

Painpoints, Objections, Benefits, Personas, Desired Outcomes, and Angles. Each linked to the next. Painpoints fed the Angles database. Personas pulled Objections by funnel stage. Benefits became messaging copy only when paired with quantified evidence.

A separate Brand Index and Knowledge Index held positioning rules and product-level facts. Field-level rules and source citations on every entry kept the data trustworthy as the team grew.

Dogfooding

Used the Passion.io platform and the YouAI product as a real customer would, twice, to inform the marketing work.

During onboarding, I built a demo app on the Passion.io platform. The point was to walk through the same flow a paying customer would, feel the friction firsthand, and bring those observations back into our messaging and onboarding work.

When the team was preparing the YouAI launch, the head of marketing asked us to spend serious time inside the product. I took the lead. Built my own coach avatar against a specific knowledge base, walked through the same setup the customer would, and audited which features the product team was emphasizing more in marketing relative to which features actually carried the user experience.

Cross-functional

Translated input from engineering, sales, design, and the founders into shipped marketing assets.

The engineering team walked me through how to build my own YouAI account, including signup, training, and the feature flags that controlled what early users saw. Discovery and sales call recordings from the sales team became the raw material I used for customer research and angle development.

During the YouAI launch period, after the founder ran a live workshop, the cross-functional team would meet to debrief, and I translated the new positioning beats from his slides into the marketing assets the same week. I partnered with the senior copywriter strategist on persona work and with the in-house customer relationship management expert on email briefs and campaign architecture.

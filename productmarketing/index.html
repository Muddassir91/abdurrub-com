Customer Research

How the YouAI personas got built.

Sat in on 20+ ICP discovery and sales calls. Took transcripts. Watched facial cues, pulled vocal patterns, sorted pain points by intensity, mapped objections to the moment they showed up in the funnel. Cross-checked the language against Reddit threads, niche forums, and creator-economy social. Output was three customer avatars with quoted limiting beliefs, sorted by buying stage.

Ask me about the methodology. The actual avatar docs and the messaging rules page are internal Passion.io property.

Strategy · Positioning

The outcome promise that was too big to convert.

YouAI's original positioning promised the ICP they could go from $100K to $10M with a single product. Cross-checked it against how Hormozi, King Kong, and the rest of the high-ticket world actually segment by revenue tier. Nobody was making that jump in one product. They were running smaller, segmented promises tied to the bracket the prospect was actually in.

Surfaced the gap. The team subdivided ICPs into revenue brackets ($100K to $300K, $300K to $1M, $1M to $3M, $3M+). Sub-$100K founders got downsold to Passion.io instead of disqualified. The change made it into a system after I left.

6 databases.
One messaging OS.
Internal Systems

The Notion stack the marketing team ran on.

Painpoints, Objections, Benefits, Personas, Desired Outcomes, and Angles. Each linked to the next. Painpoints fed Angles. Personas pulled Objections by funnel stage. Benefits surfaced as messaging only when paired with quantified evidence. Plus a Brand Index and a Knowledge Index for product-level facts. The system was the source of truth every campaign got built off.

Dogfooding

YouAI from the customer's seat.

The Head of Marketing asked the team to spend serious time inside the product. I took the lead. Created my own coach avatar against a specific knowledge base, walked through the same onboarding the user would, and audited which features the product team was over-pitching versus under-pitching relative to the actual experience. Other team members built theirs after. The audit fed directly into the launch messaging.

Dogfooding

Built my own app on Passion.io. Pinayo.app.

Part of onboarding. Built a real app on the platform, in the same flow a customer would. The topic was teaching writing. The point was not to ship publicly. The point was to feel the friction, find the spots where positioning and onboarding could be tighter, and bring those notes back into the marketing work.

Common questions

The short answers.

What does a retention strategist actually do?
A retention strategist studies how customers move through your business, finds where revenue leaks, then builds the email and lifecycle work that keeps customers buying again. The output is a system the team runs after the engagement ends, not a one-off campaign.
Who do you work with?
Businesses already past $100K with a real customer base. SaaS, DTC, services, AI products. The systems travel across all of them.
How does the engagement start?
It starts with a free retention audit on a 20-minute call. Fit gets decided in the first ten minutes, not in a long pitch. If the audit surfaces real money on the table, the next step gets discussed on the same call.
How fast does this start showing up in revenue?
First wins usually land in the first 30 days from sequences already overdue. The compounding part takes 90 to 180 days, because retention is a curve, not a campaign.

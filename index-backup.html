Who am I, and why you should trust me with your marketing.

In 2022, I was in Rs. 1,500,000 of debt, with no real plan to pay it back.

The strange part is, by every external measure I was supposed to have made it:

The first doctor in my family.

Cleared Pakistan's most competitive civil services exam on the first attempt.

I did most things right in life, but it wasn't enough. Or so I thought.

What I did next changed the trajectory of my life, and came as a surprise to everyone around me:

I chose to start a freelance marketing service online. It was a scary thought, because ~90% of my colleagues who graduated with me had gone on to seek Fellowship degrees. I was the only one not following the herd.

Three years later, I've helped move over $10 million in client revenue, $2.4 million of it for one company alone, and built marketing systems for founders, brands, and operators who now treat retention the way I do, as the cheapest growth channel they already own. And yes, I paid off all that debt.

So if I could explain my entire life in one sentence, it would be this:

I can't do average.

And I bring the same don't-settle-for-average philosophy to the work.

You can find me on socials where I write about retention systems, the math behind growing past $100K, positioning that earns its price, the inside game at AI companies, and what it actually takes to leave a respectable career and bet on yourself.

If you've stalled past $100K, or you're sitting on a list you barely email, or you're considering a hard pivot of your own, I'd like to be useful. The hardest part of the journey was figuring out the next move. The second hardest was finding the people who'd already taken it.

Common questions

The short answers.

What does a retention strategist actually do?
A retention strategist studies how customers move through your business, finds where revenue leaks, then builds the email and lifecycle work that keeps customers buying again. The output is a system the team runs after the engagement ends, not a one-off campaign.
Who do you work with?
Businesses already past $100K with a real customer base. SaaS, DTC, services, AI products. The systems travel across all of them.
How does the engagement start?
It starts with a free retention audit on a 20 minute call. Fit gets decided in the first ten minutes, not in a long pitch. If the audit surfaces real money on the table, the next step gets discussed on the same call.
Is this copywriting work?
Writing is part of the work, not the offer. The output is revenue from existing customers and leads. The inputs are research, positioning, lifecycle design, and direct response strategy.
How fast does this start showing up in revenue?
First wins usually land in the first 30 days from sequences already overdue. The compounding part takes 90 to 180 days, because retention is a curve, not a campaign.
